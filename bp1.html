Fishing the Wallkill River in Orange County

The Run Down

While I'm slightly older and smarter now, in Highschool id often fish the Wallkill River in Middletown NY by wearing a swimsuit and water shoes and wadding down river from the public access point to the Bridge. Id fish the way down and the way back up as well. This was successful for catching 8 – 12 inch smallmouths. Almost every single time I'd go in the Moring, I would catch 5-15 fish in 2-3 hours. I never caught any real gigantic fish in this spot, the largest was around 16 inches long, weighing 2-3 lbs. 14–16-inch fish were common, but it was not a guarantee to catch them.

This most common fish for me to catch out of the Wallkill is definitely small mouth bass, I’ve caught a few panfish as well, but small mouth bass are the predominant catch. I’ve head there’s walleye in the wall kill however I have not personally caught or heard of any caught. Odds are the walleye population is more abundant in other places along the river. I’ve seen a few of my buddies pull decent carp out as-well. In the end the wallkill river can make a fun and productive smallmouth fishing spot, you can catch plenty of 8-14 inch smallies

The tactics that I used to catch so many fish were to cast into breaks in the current and especially wherever the water was ripping around a large rock or a fallen tree. The tackle i would take with me consisted of a single strap pack, a Daiwa samurai 6-foot rod/reel combo with 6-8 Lb line and a Daiwa bait caster with 50lb braid. Depending on the day I would leave the bait caster in the car because honestly the smaller spinning rod was more ideal for the low hanging trees, and the lightweight tackle made catching the smaller fish more fun. On the spinning rod I would cast with inline spinner baits, Zoom Fat Alberts with split shots or a swim jig head, or Keitech Swing Impacts. The bait caster typically had topwater, a jig, or a crankbait.

How To Have The Most Success

The most efficient way I found to fish this strip of river was to use my spinning rod with an inline spinner and toss it upriver pulling it near current brakes and reeling in quickly. The little smallmouths that littered this strip would strike aggressively on fast moving lures early in the morning, while its still slightly dark out. If I caught one on a break that I could target by standing on a rock or on the shoreline I would stop and fish that same spot, often enough within a few minutes i would catch another, there was times I would catch 4-7 fish in the exact same spot in a 20–30-minute period. Usually, the more fish that I would catch in the same spot the smaller the size of them.

When the bite was slow and I would struggle to catch fish a tactic that never let me down was to throw a rooster tail or a very small crank bait. The fish were usually 8 inches so nothing large but I can’t remember a single time this let me down. On the other hand, I never had any luck throwing bottom jigs, I lost a few before I stopped throwing them, but they never caught me any fish, I attribute this to the rocky bottom that made getting snagged more likely then getting a bite.

There’s another Spot to fish from shore downriver of the bridge over the highway, I am not sure if this is a public access point or private land, I’ve never seen any posted signs or anything. This spot is located at the end of mt joy road, there’s a turn around on the left and a road blocked by a gate on the right. In front of the gate there’s a little pull off. You park there and walk about 100 yards to the river. This spot is a decent spot to catch fish from shore. At this spot swim jigs and senkos have been very productive, unfortunately this spot doesn’t produce as many fish as wadding the river, however the fish I pull here tend to be much larger then slightly up river, however they are not as abundant.

A Final Note

While I had a lot of success with my tactics i have not been to the Wallkill to fish while wadding in 5-6 years, It was brought to my attention that i was fishing down river of a water treatment plant that disinfected and returned sewage water to the Wallkill, as well as the Wallkill having issues with containing the pesticide DDT according to online sources. While I did not suffer any serious issues yet from wadding in the Wallkill, there's probably better alternatives, such as a raft and floating the river, or wearing waders and sticking to the shallow water and shorelines. I am not sure as to who owns the shorelines, however i didn't see any posted signs and believe a large chunk is government owned. Or you can fish the access point down river that i covered, its not quite as fun but still produces quality fish.
