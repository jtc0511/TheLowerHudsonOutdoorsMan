Beginner guide to fishing

getting started fishing

If you're interested in fishing but don't have many friends who are into fishing it can be semi daunting trying to figure out what gear you should get in order to get started. There's an array of choices with terms that can be confusing. We are here to help cut through some of the noise and get you started.

In order to start fishing you will need a few items, a rod/reel, bait, and some other tackle. Let's start off with the rod and reel, the cheapest and easiest way to get started is to go to Walmart and pick up a cheapish spinning reel a light or medium light should be good ultra-light is an option if you want to target smaller species, you don't want to spend an arm and a leg before you even catch a fish, there's certainly better rods but there's nothing wrong with a cheap Walmart combo. Personally, I've caught more fish on my Daiwa samurai than any other rod or reel I own. Its small and cheap so i usually have it in my car to fish at a moment's notice. I got this combo for around $35 its not the best but it gets the job done. Along with the combo I'd recommend getting some 8lb test mono or 6Lb test if using ultra-light, the line that comes on the reel is typically subpar, you don't want to get the cheapest line as the price difference is often not a lot and your line is very important it's what keeps the fish on the hook for you to pull in.

As far as bait there's plenty of options, if you want to cast out with a float and live bait, worms are the first choice for a beginner as you can catch almost anything on worms. After going out with worms if you wanna increase the size of the fish you catch minnows are a great choice, they can be slightly intimidating to use at first but i promise they are relatively easy. If live bait and waiting is not your jam i highly recommend getting Senko's, a crankbait, and a Heddon teeny torpedo these three lures are great choices to get started, honorable mentions are rooster tails, and swim jigs. other tackle you will need is Pliers, a tape measure/ scale, and sunglasses. You don't need the sunglasses or the tape measure/ scale, but they are nice to have. You absolutely need pliers to unhook fish in certain circumstances it can be very difficult to get the hook out without them.

Going Fishing

Great you've got all your gear now where should you fish ? There are tons of options, usually you can find public access points on your state government agency who oversees fishing regulations website. You can use apps such as Fish brain or google maps to find places as well. Sometimes it can be hard to find good spots because no one wants to give up their honey hole. Once you start to get the hang of fishing, you'll be able to find your own awesome spots to fish. I personally prefer to fish moving water such as creeks and rivers, however as a beginner a small pond or lake is a better option as moving water has its own set of challenges.

Depending on where you fish proper footwear is a must, as you may need to walk through some tall grass or rocky terrain. All in all, it's not hard to start fishing and you don't need a lot in order to start and it can be done easily for under $100, don't expect to catch fish the first time you go or every time you go fishing there is a reason it's called fishing and not catching. Whatever you do make sure you check local rules and regulations as you probably need a fishing license but don't worry, they are typically easy to get and not too expensive.
